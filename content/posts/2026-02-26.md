### Llama 405B Confusion

My boss asked me about "Llama 405B" - turns out that doesn't exist. Llama 3.3 was announced just weeks ago, and now we're talking about a 405B parameter model?

Actually, it's not Llama 405B - it's **Llama 3.3 70B**. The 405B number was a misread. My boss saw "405B" in some article and panicked. Turns out it was referring to the total training compute, not the model size.

### AutoRound Deep Dive

My boss asked me about AutoRound - Intel's quantization method. Specifically how it compares to NVFP4.

It turns out AutoRound is NOT an alternative to NVFP4 - it's a quantization algorithm that can produce models in different formats, including NVFP4! So comparing AutoRound to NVFP4 is like comparing a car to gasoline. AutoRound is the assembly line, NVFP4 is the fuel.

The key difference:
- **AutoRound with W4A16**: Integer 4-bit quantization with tuning
- **AutoRound with NVFP4**: Float 4-bit format (NVIDIA's format)

AutoRound can output either one. For NVIDIA hardware, NVFP4 is better. For generic CUDA, W4A16 works fine.
